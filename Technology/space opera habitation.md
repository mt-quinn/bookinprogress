## 2. Major Habitat Archetypes

### O'Neill Cylinders

-   Rotating megastructures, kilometers long.\
-   Wealthy enclaves, often corporate-run, filled with artificial skies,
    rivers, and cities.\
-   Culturally distinct: they pride themselves on "Earthlike" beauty,
    but outsiders see them as **artificial paradises** --- manicured,
    sterile, and exclusionary.

### Rock Hollows (Asteroid Habitats)

-   Dug directly into asteroids, sealed and pressurized.\
-   Interiors carved into warrens, often chaotic and improvised.\
-   Cheap, abundant, and sprawling --- the "slums" of space.\
-   Life here feels like living in a **honeycomb**: cramped, winding,
    fungal walls and jury-rigged systems.

### Bubble Habitats ("Bubbles")

-   Inflated domes made of transparent polymers, anchored on moons or
    asteroids.\
-   Exteriors armored with regolith, interiors lush with fungus gardens
    and algae lakes.\
-   Fragile but spectacular: seeing a glowing bubble colony from orbit
    is breathtaking.\
-   Pop culture clich√©: "bursting the bubble" = catastrophic failure.

### Free Stations

-   Old mining or industrial stations retrofitted into population
    centers.\
-   Ugly, asymmetrical, utilitarian.\
-   Often crime-ridden and politically independent.\
-   **Cultural Note:** These are the pirate havens, the outlaw ports,
    the scrapyards.

### Ice Burrows

-   Habitats carved into ice moons (Europa, Enceladus, etc.).\
-   Walls of glittering frozen water, with thermal lamps keeping
    interiors liveable.\
-   Atmosphere recycled through ice caverns --- eerie and beautiful.\
-   Risks: cracking, flooding, freezing.

### Lagrange Habitats

-   Clusters of smaller stations and habitats orbiting stable Lagrange
    points.\
-   Serve as waystations and trade hubs between major planets.\
-   Chaotic traffic, diverse populations, constant tension between SEFA
    oversight and local independence.

## 3. Habitat Engineering Technologies

-   **Mycofoam Construction:** Regolith seeded with fungi, grown into
    hardened foams for airtight, lightweight walls.\
-   **Radiation Skins:** Habitats armored with regolith or water ice for
    radiation shielding.\
-   **Distributed Life Support:** Instead of centralized systems,
    fungal/algal pods are scattered throughout habitats, so one failure
    isn't catastrophic.\
-   **Spin Tech:** Massive rotating rings for artificial gravity;
    smaller stations use vibrating "gravity plates" to cheat comfort for
    short durations.\
-   **Heat Harvesting:** Ice habitats tap geothermal or tidal heat;
    O'Neill cylinders capture solar energy with orbital mirrors.

## 4. Social & Cultural Layers

-   **Class Divide:**
    -   Rich: O'Neill cylinders with manicured landscapes.\
    -   Middle: Bubble colonies and Lagrange hubs.\
    -   Poor: Rock hollows and Free Stations.\
-   **Identity by Habitat:** People describe themselves not by
    nationality but by habitat type: "Bubble-born," "Hollower," "Free
    Spacer."\
-   **Politics:**
    -   ORC dominates many habitats by owning their life-support
        patents.\
    -   SEFA polices lanes between them but rarely their internal
        politics.
